Pfizer For All

Vaccine Education Experience

Overview

Pfizer For All set out to become a trusted place for everyday health information. As vaccine misinformation and hesitancy rose, we needed to build more than static content ‚Äî we needed an experience that helped people feel informed, reassured, and motivated to take action.

By combining interactive modules, quizzes, soft nudges, long-form content, and a searchable knowledge hub, we created an ecosystem that met people where they were ‚Äî from the hesitant to the proactive.

My Role & Impact

I led end-to-end design, from synthesizing research to final specs. Every decision was grounded in user needs, behavioral data, and iterative testing.

I collaborated daily with research, product, content, and engineering to align on goals, move fast, and ensure what we built was clear, accessible, and trustworthy.

The Problem

Users were overwhelmed and confused:

  • ‚Ä¢ They didn't know which vaccines applied to them
  • ‚Ä¢ Misinformation created fear and inaction
  • ‚Ä¢ Old pages were dense and static, with no clear next step

Behavioral insight: People wanted clear, credible answers, but delivered in a tone that felt human and supportive ‚Äî not overwhelming.

"I just want to know what's right for me, without feeling pressured."

‚Äî Research participant

The Solution

We designed a layered experience:

‚úÖ

Interactive vaccine pages

Short quizzes, myth-busting cards, and scenarios to gently guide hesitant users

‚úÖ

Soft nudges

Clear, timely CTAs to check eligibility or find local vaccines ‚Äî always optional, never pushy

‚úÖ

Long-form content

Detailed explainers in plain language for those who want to go deeper

‚úÖ

Knowledge hub

A trusted library people could return to anytime for up-to-date answers

Process

Discovery

We partnered with research to map audience segments ‚Äî from the hesitant to the proactive. Through interviews and surveys, we learned what built confidence and where people got stuck.

Ideation

I facilitated workshops to reimagine static content. How could a static page become a guided conversation? Where could we use quizzes or myth-busting to gently correct misinformation?

Low-Fidelity Design

We tested flows for both skimmers and deep divers. Wireframes showed how interactive elements and long-form articles could connect naturally.

High-Fidelity Design

I designed clean, modular pages with clear hierarchy and accessible typography. Every component ‚Äî quizzes, inline fact cards, related reads ‚Äî felt part of the same trusted experience.

Testing

We ran multiple rounds of usability testing with diverse users. Insights led to clearer quiz flows, more reassuring microcopy, and CTAs that felt like gentle next steps ‚Äî not sales pitches.

Working with Engineering

I delivered detailed specs and edge-case states. Close collaboration ensured the knowledge hub search, responsive layouts, and interactive elements worked smoothly for every user.
