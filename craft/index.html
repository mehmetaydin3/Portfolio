The Brief

A marketplace for experiences that feel personal, not transactional.

Craft is a NYC-based startup marketplace for booking tickets to classes, projects, experiences, and lessons offered by craft professionals. The platform targets users seeking personable experiences in intimate settings — positioning itself against competitors like Airbnb Experiences and Sofar Sounds.

I was tasked with designing a responsive website (desktop, tablet, mobile) that enables users to search for experiences, complete bookings, and leave reviews — with modern branding and a mobile-first philosophy.

Phase 01

Research

Research started with market analysis and competitive positioning against key players in the experience-booking space. I created provisional personas based on early assumptions, then validated through interviews with 5 participants who had all booked experiences online within the last 3 months.

Market analysis and competitive positioning against Airbnb Experiences, Sofar Sounds, and other players

Research Interviews

I spent time with each participant uncovering how they discover, evaluate, and book experiences. The findings were consistent: social discovery matters, hidden fees are a dealbreaker, and users expect modern payment options and e-ticket confirmation.

"I learn something new about myself with each experience." — Research participant

Key Findings

Users discover experiences primarily through friends and social media. They expect e-tickets and confirmation emails. Hidden fees and charges revealed at checkout create significant frustration. They prefer modern, fast interfaces with multiple payment options (Venmo, PayPal).

Core Problem Statement

Experience-booking platforms fail users at the moment of commitment — hidden fees, confusing checkout flows, and discovery limited to algorithmic recommendations. Craft needs to earn trust from the first search to the final confirmation.

Phase 02

Define

Interview insights were synthesized into an empathy map, then distilled into a primary persona — Sharon — who represents the core user: someone who seeks meaningful, social experiences but is burned by friction and lack of transparency in existing platforms.

Phase 03

Ideate

The ideation phase merged user research needs with business goals and technical constraints through a Venn diagram exercise. A user journey map captured the key booking stages with emotional touchpoints and interview quotes integrated throughout. The user flow then mapped the happy path for booking an experience.

Venn diagram — merging user research insights with business objectives and technical constraints

User journey map — booking stages with persona perspective, interview quotes, and emotional touchpoints

User flow — happy path for discovering and completing an experience booking

The Solution Shape

A mobile-first booking platform with transparent pricing, social discovery, and a clean checkout flow. Product cards are modular, memorable, and consistent — the trust layer is built through design before a user ever reaches checkout.

Transparency from first search to final confirmation

Phase 04

Prototype

Initial low-fidelity sketches explored layout and aesthetic concepts, with desktop sketches informing the mobile-first wireframe development. Mid-fidelity wireframes established annotated component patterns with a focus on modular, consistent product cards.

Low-fidelity sketches — exploring aesthetic and layout concepts before moving to digital wireframes

Mid-fidelity wireframes — annotated component patterns and modular product card system

Color, typography, and imagery were then applied to the wireframes with minimal layout reorganization. The interactive prototype showcases the complete checkout flow including a floating toolbar, sticky search, sticky footer CTA, and date/time selection.

High-fidelity UI — clean aesthetic with transparent pricing and social proof integrated throughout

Phase 05

Outcome

100% task completion. Users praised the simple, modern design and all 5 participants expressed genuine interest in using the platform. The transparency-first design approach resolved the trust friction identified in research.

Reflection

Design earns trust before checkout ever appears.

The biggest insight from Craft was that trust isn't built at the payment screen — it's built in every decision that comes before it. Card layouts, price transparency, social proof, and response time signals all communicate trustworthiness before a user ever reaches checkout.

Future iterations should expand features through additional research. A separate persona and design track for the artist/experience creator side of the platform would unlock the supply-side dynamic that makes marketplaces work.

What I Would Explore Next

The creator-facing platform — how experience providers list, manage, and promote their sessions. A review and rating system designed around authenticity rather than vanity metrics. And a social layer that surfaces what friends have attended or saved.
