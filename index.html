Pfizer For All

Vaccine Education Experience

Overview

Pfizer For All set out to become a trusted place for everyday health information. As vaccine misinformation and hesitancy rose, we needed to build more than static content — we needed an experience that helped people feel informed, reassured, and motivated to take action.

By combining interactive modules, quizzes, soft nudges, long-form content, and a searchable knowledge hub, we created an ecosystem that met people where they were — from the hesitant to the proactive.

My Role & Impact

I led end-to-end design, from synthesizing research to final specs. Every decision was grounded in user needs, behavioral data, and iterative testing.

I collaborated daily with research, product, content, and engineering to align on goals, move fast, and ensure what we built was clear, accessible, and trustworthy.

The Problem

Users were overwhelmed and confused:

  • • They didn't know which vaccines applied to them
  • • Misinformation created fear and inaction
  • • Old pages were dense and static, with no clear next step

Behavioral insight: People wanted clear, credible answers, but delivered in a tone that felt human and supportive — not overwhelming.

"I just want to know what's right for me, without feeling pressured."

— Research participant

The Solution

We designed a layered experience:

✅

Interactive vaccine pages

Short quizzes, myth-busting cards, and scenarios to gently guide hesitant users

✅

Soft nudges

Clear, timely CTAs to check eligibility or find local vaccines — always optional, never pushy

✅

Long-form content

Detailed explainers in plain language for those who want to go deeper

✅

Knowledge hub

A trusted library people could return to anytime for up-to-date answers

Process

Discovery

We partnered with research to map audience segments — from the hesitant to the proactive. Through interviews and surveys, we learned what built confidence and where people got stuck.

Ideation

I facilitated workshops to reimagine static content. How could a static page become a guided conversation? Where could we use quizzes or myth-busting to gently correct misinformation?

Low-Fidelity Design

We tested flows for both skimmers and deep divers. Wireframes showed how interactive elements and long-form articles could connect naturally.

High-Fidelity Design

I designed clean, modular pages with clear hierarchy and accessible typography. Every component — quizzes, inline fact cards, related reads — felt part of the same trusted experience.

Testing

We ran multiple rounds of usability testing with diverse users. Insights led to clearer quiz flows, more reassuring microcopy, and CTAs that felt like gentle next steps — not sales pitches.

Working with Engineering

I delivered detailed specs and edge-case states. Close collaboration ensured the knowledge hub search, responsive layouts, and interactive elements worked smoothly for every user.

📌 BCG Smart Search — Internal Knowledge Platform Redesign

Overview

Boston Consulting Group's Smart Search is a mission-critical internal platform used daily by thousands of consultants to access BCG's deep library of documents, case studies, and institutional knowledge. Over time, the platform had become visually outdated, cluttered, and inefficient — frustrating consultants and forcing them to waste valuable time finding trusted information.

Goal: Modernize Smart Search to reflect BCG's premium brand, improve relevance and speed, introduce personalization, and create an intuitive feedback loop that helps the system learn and improve over time.

My Role & Team

I was a Product Designer on a cross-functional team including a Lead Designer, Product Manager, Strategist, Developers, and BCG Consultants. I contributed to research, journey mapping, UX and UI design, prototyping, and usability testing — always grounding decisions in real consultant workflows and daily needs.

Key Challenges

Through surveys, interviews, and live shadowing, we uncovered four major pain points:

• Outdated visual design

Did not align with BCG's modern, premium brand.

• Low relevance

Inefficient algorithms and poor ranking logic returned too many irrelevant results.

• Cluttered UX

Dense layouts and hidden filters increased cognitive load.

• No feedback loop

Consultants had no easy way to flag poor results and help improve search accuracy.

Design Principles

We defined four guiding principles for the redesign:

✅

Trust & Relevance

Deliver results that consultants feel confident using.

✅

Modern Simplicity

Create a clean, elegant UI that feels fresh and easy to scan.

✅

Personalization

Adapt to each user's unique workflows and needs.

✅

Continuous Improvement

Build in clear tools for users to give feedback and improve the system.

Before: Previous Design (Before I Joined)

The existing interface before I took over - cluttered layout, poor information hierarchy, and low result relevance

After: Redesigned Search Results

Clean, organized results with improved relevance and filtering

Solutions & Rationale

🔍

Smarter, More Relevant Search

Why: Consultants spent too much time digging through irrelevant results.

What we did: Improved ranking algorithms, added advanced filters and concept suggestions, and redesigned result previews with richer metadata.

Backed by: Usability testing showed a clear improvement in perceived relevance — with around 45% more users finding what they needed on the first try compared to the original version.

🎨

Modern, Simplified UX

Why: The old interface felt cluttered and dated, eroding trust.

What we did: Established clear visual hierarchy, added sticky advanced filters for easy refinement, and redesigned the UI to align with BCG's premium brand.

Backed by: Consultants in testing described the redesign as "more modern, clear, and professional" and found tasks quicker and less cognitively demanding.

✨

Personalization

Why: Consultants often repeated the same searches day after day.

What we did: Introduced personalized dashboards with recent activity, frequently accessed documents, and smart recommendations based on individual usage.

Backed by: Feedback showed consultants felt the system now "learns with me," helping them stay efficient.

💬

Continuous Feedback Loop

Why: The system couldn't evolve without input from real users.

What we did: Designed intuitive in-line tools for consultants to rate and flag search results — encouraging quick, easy micro-feedback that trains the system over time.

Backed by: Early rollout showed strong adoption and gave our team valuable data to further refine search performance.

Process Highlights

•

Discovery: Surveys, in-depth interviews, and live shadowing to map real workflows.

•

Wireframing & Prototyping: Low-fidelity wireframes to test new flows early and build stakeholder confidence.

•

Testing & Iteration: Multiple usability rounds with consultants, each informing new refinements.

•

Collaboration: Weekly syncs with developers, strategists, and BCG stakeholders to ensure technical feasibility and business alignment.

Impact

The redesigned Smart Search is more than just a facelift — it's a modern, intelligent, and elegant tool that consultants trust and enjoy using:

Task-based testing showed significantly faster completion times.
