+play — Subscription Marketplace

Lead Designer · E2E Low to High-Fidelity Design · UX Research · Executive Presentations · VQA & Accessibility Testing

Launched · Oscar Campaign Millions of users nationally 30+ partners

+Play is Verizon's one-stop-shop to discover, purchase and save on subscription services, featuring over 30 partners spanning from entertainment to education, lifestyle to fitness and more.

The Challenge

Managing a growing list of subscriptions across diverse categories is a pain point for many consumers. Verizon saw an opportunity to empower their customers with a centralized solution.

My Role

As Lead Designer, I spearheaded the creation of +play — Verizon's one-stop subscription hub. I led extensive user research and collaborated with Product and Marketing teams to design a platform for easy subscription discovery, purchase, and management.

+play — the plan-aware subscription marketplace, with personalized recommendations and editorial content

Marketplace browse — curated partner catalog with category filters, pricing tiers, and plan-based eligibility

Left: 1-click checkout — subscription live before leaving the page. Right: Unified manage hub — subscriptions, billing, credits in one view

Notification system — renewal reminders, new partner offers, and plan perk alerts keep users engaged post-activation

Impact

Launched with a high-profile campaign featured at the Oscars, +Play generated mass media coverage and rapid customer adoption. Millions of users nationwide rely on +Play for its ease of use, streamlined shopping with one-click checkout, and personalized recommendations, helping them save on subscriptions they enjoy.

User feedback highlights the platform's success in simplifying subscription management, driving high user engagement and satisfaction. +play continues to evolve, incorporating ongoing user feedback and market trends to remain at the forefront of subscription management solutions for Verizon.

Verizon Assistant

Conversational Chatbot Interface · Conversational Design System Expansion Pack · Launched 2023

Reduced call volume Improved NPS Global design system

An intelligent chatbot to streamline customer needs with automation — integrated directly into the My Verizon app to handle billing, credits, payment changes, and routine tasks that previously required frustrating in-store visits or long hold times.

The Challenge

A surge in customer service calls flooded Verizon, tackling issues like billing, credits, payment changes, and simple tasks like payments — often requiring frustrating in-store visits. This led to customer dissatisfaction and operational inefficiency.

The Solution

As Lead Designer, I spearheaded the end-to-end design of the Verizon Assistant — a conversational AI tool integrated into the My Verizon app, working closely with cross-functional teams across Product, Development, Copywriting, Strategy, and Design System.

Two Parallel Workstreams

  • Design System for Conversational UI: We built a comprehensive design system for conversational UI elements, ensuring consistency and ease of use for the Assistant across the globe.
  • User-Centric Design for Critical Flows: We prioritized designing for the most critical user flows, focusing on tasks like billing inquiries, credits, and payment changes — simplified and streamlined so customers could handle them independently.

Verizon Assistant design system documentation — conversation components, states, intents, and interaction patterns

Impact

  • Reduced Frustration: Simplified processes meant users could easily handle common tasks themselves.
  • Improved Customer Satisfaction: Empowerment and reduced wait times led to happier customers.
  • Operational Efficiency: Reduced call volume freed up resources, allowing Verizon to focus on more complex issues.

Call Filter

E2E App Design · Launched 2020

−30% reported spam calls E2E redesign

Helping customers easily identify suspicious callers while reducing spam calls. I led a complete redesign of Call Filter, focusing on user-friendliness and ways to integrate smart spam identification that users can come to trust and adopt as a preventative measure for obtrusive phone calls.

The goal was to imagine a user interface that not only prevented spam calls effectively but also alerted customers in a clear and informative way — turning a passive block into an active, trusted guardian.

Design Approach

We wanted to build trust through transparency. Rather than a black-box blocker, the redesign surfaced why a call was flagged, what risk level it carried, and gave users control over their own filters — without requiring technical knowledge.

Outcome

Through research, design, and stakeholder buy-in, this redesign empowered users and reduced reported spam calls by 30%. The new interface became a trusted daily utility rather than a background setting users forgot about.

Call Filter redesign — clear risk categorization, transparent flagging, and user-controlled filter settings

"A spam blocker only works if people trust it. We designed trust first." — Design principle, Call Filter redesign

Retail Touchtable

E2E Retail Concept Design · Piloting 2024

Currently piloting in-store 43" interactive touchscreen First-of-its-kind

A representative-assisted selling tool to showcase the portfolio of Verizon offerings — a 43-inch interactive touchscreen that bridges the gap between rep-assisted selling and digital channels, bringing the full depth of Verizon's product suite to a single collaborative surface.

The Challenge

  • Confusion for Customers: The sheer variety of offerings led to difficulty selecting the best plan.
  • Limited Rep Communication: Reps struggled to showcase the full value proposition and customize plans line-by-line.
  • Subscription Awareness: Customers often remained unaware of available a la carte subscription services.

The Solution

  • Line & Account Level Add-Ons: Clear visual differentiation separates line-level add-ons from account-level options like entertainment bundles.
  • Building the Perfect Plan: User-friendly interface lets reps tailor plans based on individual needs.
  • Simplifying Complexity: Clear information architecture and interactive elements empower informed decisions.

Retail Touchtable — plan builder interface showing line-level add-ons, device selection, and bundle recommendations

In-store pilot — the 43" touchscreen in a live Verizon retail environment, enabling rep-assisted collaborative selling

Impact

  • Improved Customer Understanding: Customers gain a clearer picture of Verizon's product portfolio and the benefits of bundled services.
  • Enhanced Rep Efficiency: Reps can effectively communicate value propositions and achieve customized plan building without back-and-forth.
  • Bridging the Gap: This innovative solution integrates the strengths of rep-assisted selling and digital exploration, creating a more seamless customer journey.

Retail Check-in Kiosk

E2E Design · Launched 2024 · Rolled out nationally

Rolled out nationally Reduced wait times Improved rep focus

Helping customers easily check in to their in-store appointments while reducing wait times. The kiosk serves as the first point of contact for customers entering a Verizon store — streamlining appointment check-in, walk-in registration, and order pick-up without any rep intervention required.

The Challenge

Verizon retail stores faced challenges managing customer flow, appointment scheduling, and walk-in wait times. Customers often experienced confusion and frustration, while representatives were distracted from actual selling by check-in processes.

Design Approach

Working with CX&I, we ran an Ideation Workshop to brainstorm solutions to optimize customer flow. The result: a simple, fast experience using clear and concise design with intuitive prompts — check-in, register, or pick up within minutes.

Check-in Kiosk — appointment check-in, walk-in registration, and real-time waitlist visibility in a single kiosk interface

Key Design Decisions

  • Simple & Fast Experience: Clear prompts guide customers through check-in, walk-in registration, or order pick-up — all within minutes.
  • Real-Time Visibility: The kiosk displays a live waitlist, allowing customers to manage their expectations and see estimated waiting time.
  • Improved Rep Efficiency: By streamlining check-in, reps are freed from distractions and can pre-prepare for each customer based on their chosen service — further enhancing the overall experience.

Impact

  • Reduced Wait Times: Customers experience shorter wait times through efficient self-service options.
  • Improved Customer Satisfaction: Streamlined processes create a more positive and predictable in-store experience.
  • Enhanced Rep Focus: Representatives devote more time to individual customer needs, boosting service quality across every store.

Reflection

What 4.5 years of designing at Verizon scale taught me.

The through-line across every project — +play, the Assistant, Call Filter, the Touchtable, the Kiosk — is the same: Verizon's scale means that every design decision, no matter how small, touches millions of people. The pressure to get it right is matched only by the opportunity to make something genuinely useful at a scale most designers never encounter.

Working across digital, conversational, and physical surfaces in the same engagement sharpened a systems-level fluency that's hard to build anywhere else. When the same brand logic, the same accessibility bar, and the same customer trust equation has to hold in an app, a chatbot, and a 43-inch touchscreen simultaneously — it fundamentally changes how you think about design.

+play's Oscars launch — a high-profile campaign that introduced the platform to millions and set the tone for its continued growth

These five projects represent different surfaces, different users, and different business goals — but a single shared conviction: that design is how Verizon keeps its promise to customers.

Getting that right, consistently, across physical and digital channels at carrier scale, is the work I'm most proud of.
