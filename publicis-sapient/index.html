Context & Engagement

Verizon had millions of subscription perks. No one could find them.

Verizon wireless plans came bundled with streaming perks — Netflix, Disney+, Apple Music, and dozens more. But these benefits were scattered across one-off landing pages, buried in billing flows, and advertised in disconnected marketing campaigns. Customers had no single place to discover, activate, or manage what they were already paying for.

Publicis Sapient brought me in as Lead Product Designer to fix this. The mandate was clear: design +play — a unified subscription marketplace that lived inside the Verizon ecosystem and made plan benefits the centerpiece of the experience.

My role and the cross-functional team at Publicis Sapient + Verizon

The Problem

Subscriptions were everywhere. Management was nowhere.

The average Verizon customer held 5+ streaming and digital subscriptions — each with its own billing cycle, cancellation flow, and app. The perks they earned through their wireless plan were virtually invisible: buried in promo emails, spread across disconnected pages, dependent on call center support to redeem.

Discovery was accidental. Redemption was frustrating. Management was impossible. The result was massive partner activation loss and a support burden that should never have existed.

Subscriptions scattered across billing, marketing, and loyalty — with no unified layer

"I didn't even know I had Netflix through Verizon." — User research insight

User research revealed systematic confusion — disconnected systems, missed activations, high call center dependency

Core problems to solve

Disconnected systems: loyalty, billing, and marketing operated as separate silos with no shared UX layer. Partner activations (Netflix, HBO, Disney) were being missed at massive scale. There was no unified view of what a customer owned, what they were eligible for, or what they were paying for. High dependency on call centers for redemption eroded both NPS and partner revenue.

Product Vision

One marketplace. Every subscription. Plan-aware.

+play had to be more than a catalog. The vision was a plan-aware platform that understood each user's Verizon tier — surfacing relevant perks automatically, calculating plan-specific pricing, and enabling activation without leaving the ecosystem.

We designed two north stars: what the platform needed to feel like for a brand-new user on day one, and what it needed to evolve into for a returning user who had built a subscription history.

New user north star — personalized welcome, bundled perks surfaced immediately, 1-tap activation

Returning user state — behavior-driven offers, active subscriptions always visible, non-transactional tone

How the platform worked

A user's Verizon plan tier determines everything they see. Eligible perks surface automatically on the homepage. Subscriptions they already own elsewhere can be migrated and consolidated under a single Verizon bill. New activations happen through a single-screen checkout — billing pre-filled through Verizon, no credentials, no redirect, no friction.

Every touchpoint — discovery, PDP, checkout, migration, management — is shaped by the plan relationship. A generic marketplace becomes a personalized subscription hub.

MVP prioritization — fast activation, plan perks, modular feeds, migration, and homepage trust

The full platform scope: Homepage, PDP, 1-click activation, migration, management, and personalized feed

Product Design Decisions

The platform set the rules. The interface had to make them invisible.

01 — Homepage & Discovery

A marketplace that knows your plan

The homepage isn't a static catalog. It's a dynamic surface shaped by the user's plan tier. Contextual offers, trending content, and personalized recommendations replace a generic grid — surfacing what's relevant before users go looking for it. Bundled perks appear as activated offers, not buried footnotes.

+play Homepage — dynamic, plan-aware content hierarchy across mobile and desktop

02 — Subscription PDP & 1-Click Activation

From browsing to subscribed in a single screen

Traditional multi-step checkout flows were eliminated. The PDP combines content preview, plan-specific pricing, and 1-click activation into a single screen. Verizon handles billing automatically — no credential entry, no payment forms, no redirects. The subscription is live before the user leaves the page.

+52% checkout conversion

PDP to checkout in one flow — accordion details, saved wallet, clear pricing, migration notice

03 — Subscription Migration

Move your subscriptions in — without losing a day of access

Users with existing Netflix, HBO Max, or Hulu subscriptions could migrate them into +play without interruption or double-billing. The flow was designed to feel like an upgrade — not a chore. Multi-select, onboarding guidance, sticky CTAs, and automatic billing transfer made the transition invisible.

Migration experience — multi-select transfer, onboarding tooltips, returning user activation state

04 — Unified Management Hub

Every subscription. One bill. Full control.

A three-tab architecture — Subscriptions, Billing & Credits, and Offers — gives users a single hub for everything they own, owe, and could access. Active subscriptions with renewal dates, consolidated billing history, and exclusive member offers all live in one place. What was previously spread across five apps and two support channels now lives in a single, scannable view.

15M+ subscriptions managed

Manage hub — active subscriptions with clear renewal dates, monthly billing summary, exclusive member offers

Spotlight Feature

Discovery should feel like browsing, not searching.

The Discover tab reimagines entertainment discovery as a content-forward experience. Instead of a grid of logos, users browse through curated editorial categories, trending content, Oscar winners, and personalized recommendations — then find exactly where to watch.

The experience mirrors how people actually find new entertainment: through context and mood, not keyword queries. Category-based exploration, visual content cards, and a search-to-subscription pipeline replaced the traditional catalog browse.

"Discovery should surface intent before users know they have it." — Design principle, +play Discover

Discover — editorial landing, search-to-subscription pipeline, content detail with direct activation
